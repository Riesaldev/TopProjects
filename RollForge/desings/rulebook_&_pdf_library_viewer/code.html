Gods of Your World

Chapter 1 | A World of Your Own

The gods of your world are not distant concepts; they are active forces that shape history and the land itself. When creating your pantheon, consider how these deities interact with mortals. Do they walk among them in disguise, or do they rule from distant planes?

In a fantasy setting, religion is rarely a matter of faith alone. Clerics channel divine magic, paladins swear sacred oaths, and druids draw power from nature itself. These are tangible proofs of the divine.

Divine Ranks

Gods typically fall into a hierarchy. Greater deities (search match) possess immense power and influence, often overseeing broad concepts like War, Love, or Death. Lesser deities might control more specific domains, such as a single river or the craft of weaving.

Pantheon Sample: The Dawn War

  • Bahamut: Justice, Nobility, Protection
  • Corellon: Arcane Magic, Spring, Beauty
  • Erathis: Civilization, Invention, Law
  • Ioun: Knowledge, Skill, Prophecy
Statue of a forgotten deity

Quasi-deities have a divine origin but lack the full power of a god. They might be children of gods and mortals, or mortals who have ascended through legendary deeds.

Titans are the primordial creators or destroyers, often predating the gods themselves. Their power is raw and elemental.

Religious Organizations

Temples act as centers of community life as much as places of worship. In small villages, a shrine might be dedicated to a local spirit, while in great cities, cathedrals to the major gods dominate the skyline.
